There are 630 million women in India.

88% of women and girls in india have no access to sanitary pads.

1 of 3 girls will drop out of school upon puberty.

The Problem

In order for women and girls to move out of poverty they need three simple things:
health, safety, & an education.
For many girls in India, the only thing standing in their way is a simply manufactured collection of cotton,
a pad.

There are more than
250 million girls living in poverty
worldwide. In India alone, cutting the dropout rate in half would give
opportunities
to more than 107 million women.

Sustainable Solution & Process

Betr pads produced using our machines are easily created from simple, raw materials and provide a
healthy, hygienic
way to manage menstruation. Many alternatives currently used by women around the world can cause serious, sometimes deadly, side effects and create shame around women’s bodies and its processes.

Not only does the Betr process meet an immediate need for a product, it is a sustainable model. It’s process includes limited machinery using raw materials, locally sourced and provided for a low cost. But most importantly, it provides women with an
opportunity for entrepreneurship
in developing nations and a living wage.

Impact Through Empowerment & Entrepreneurship

The lack of access to basic healthcare is the largest contributing factor to girls in India not continuing education past the age of puberty. In places where girls have access to school this simple, decades old technology should not stand in their way.

Each Betr machine can produce enough pads for an entire village. The women learn the process of manufacturing and how to run a business. It creates a community of women where their bodies are treated with normalcy.
They no longer have to hide a natural process experienced by every woman in the world.
